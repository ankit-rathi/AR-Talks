Your instinct is correct. If you start with foundational topics like “What is Data?” or “Reality → Observations → Data,” you will build intellectual purity but not distribution momentum. The younger audience discovers creators through curiosity, surprise, relevance, fear, status, or confusion — not through foundational sequencing.

You should therefore separate your strategy into two layers:

```text
Audience Acquisition Layer
↓
Systems Understanding Layer
```

The first layer maximizes reach.

The second layer builds long-term intellectual authority.

The mistake would be starting too academically. People do not wake up wondering about “structured vs unstructured data.” They wake up wondering:

```text
How ChatGPT works
Why AI is suddenly everywhere
Will AI replace jobs
Why Netflix knows them
What AI agents are
Why AI hallucinates
Why companies collect data
```

That means your initial reels should attack:

* high curiosity,
* high confusion,
* high emotional energy,
* high relatability,
* high current relevance.

Then later you connect those viral concepts backward into the deeper system map.

Your best starting strategy is:

```text
Viral Buzzword
↓
Hidden Mechanism
↓
Systems Thinking
↓
Dependency Mapping
↓
Mental Model
```

This means your first 50–100 reels should NOT follow book order.

They should follow:

```text
Attention Order
then
Dependency Order
```

Now another important insight.

Your reels should NOT feel like:

* lectures,
* tutorials,
* definitions,
* textbooks.

They should feel like:

```text
Hidden Reality Reveals
```

The strongest hooks are:

* “You think X… but actually…”
* “Every time you use X…”
* “The scary thing about AI is…”
* “Nobody explains this properly…”
* “This is why AI companies collect your data…”
* “This invisible system runs modern companies…”

That creates:

* curiosity tension,
* emotional engagement,
* cognitive surprise.

For example:

```text
“You think ChatGPT is thinking.

It’s not.

It’s predicting the next word
using statistical patterns learned
from massive amounts of human text.

That single idea explains
almost the entire AI revolution.”
```

That is:

* short,
* visual,
* surprising,
* intuitive,
* memorable.

Exactly what short-form algorithms reward.
