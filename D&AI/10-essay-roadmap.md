# Your Essay Structure (Version 2.0)

Instead of sections, think in terms of a learning journey.

| Stage                         | Purpose                                                                      | Reader Experience                            |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **1. The Puzzle**             | Present a real situation with tension.                                       | "I've seen this happen."                     |
| **2. Let Them Think**         | Ask the central question without answering it.                               | "Why does this happen?"                      |
| **3. The Investigation**      | Explore common explanations and why they only partially explain the problem. | "Those explanations don't fully satisfy me." |
| **4. The Historical Journey** | Show how today's situation evolved over time.                                | "Now I understand how we got here."          |
| **5. The Aha! Moment**        | Introduce your new lens or mental model.                                     | "I never thought about it that way."         |
| **6. Build Confidence**       | Show readers how they can apply the new lens elsewhere.                      | "I can use this way of thinking."            |
| **7. Leave Them Curious**     | End with an open question rather than a perfect conclusion.                  | "Now I'm wondering..."                       |

This structure embodies your five teaching principles:

* Start with the problem.
* Build toward discovery.
* Let the reader wrestle with it.
* Deliver a meaningful insight.
* Equip them to think differently.

## One suggestion that I think could define your work

I'd encourage you to rename these from **essays** to **investigations**.

That single word changes the reader's expectation.

You're no longer saying:

> "Here is what I think."

You're saying:

> "Let's investigate something together."

That fits beautifully with your teaching philosophy. It invites readers into a process of discovery, aligns with your emphasis on intellectual humility, and reinforces your goal of helping professionals develop better mental models rather than simply absorbing conclusions. If the investigations consistently lead readers from a familiar puzzle to a deeper way of thinking, you'll create a body of work that is both educational and memorable.
