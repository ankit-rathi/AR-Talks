Here are comprehensive study notes based on the lecture for Class 10 History: **Nationalism in India**. These notes cover the evolution of the national movement from 1915 onwards.

### 1. The Impact of World War I
The First World War created a new economic and political situation in India:
*   **Economic Distress:** The British increased **defense expenditure**, leading to higher taxes and price hikes for essential goods.
*   **Forced Recruitment:** Villagers were forcibly recruited into the British army, causing widespread anger.
*   **Crops and Health:** Crop failures and the spread of diseases (famines) worsened the situation, for which the British were held responsible.

### 2. The Idea of Satyagraha
**Mahatma Gandhi** returned from South Africa in **January 1915**. 
*   **Definition:** Satyagraha emphasizes the **power of truth** and the need to search for it. Gandhi argued that if the cause was true, physical force was not necessary to fight the oppressor; victory could be won through **non-violence**.
*   **Early Successes:**
    *   **1917:** Champaran (Bihar) – for indigo plantation workers.
    *   **1917:** Kheda (Gujarat) – for peasants affected by crop failure.
    *   **1918:** Ahmedabad – for cotton mill workers.

### 3. The Rowlatt Act and Jallianwala Bagh (1919)
*   **Rowlatt Act:** Passed by the Imperial Legislative Council, this "Black Law" allowed the government to **detain political prisoners without trial for two years**.
*   **Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (April 13, 1919):** After martial law was imposed, General Dyer fired upon a peaceful crowd gathered for the Baisakhi fair in Amritsar. This led to widespread violence, and Gandhi called off the movement.

### 4. Non-Cooperation Movement (NCM) - 1921
Gandhi believed British rule survived only because of Indian cooperation. In his book ***Hind Swaraj***, he argued that if Indians refused to cooperate, British rule would collapse.
*   **The Khilafat Issue:** To bring **Hindus and Muslims** together, Gandhi supported the Khilafat movement, which protested the harsh treatment of the Ottoman Emperor (the Caliph).
*   **Program of NCM:** Surrender of titles, boycott of civil services, army, police, courts, and foreign goods.
*   **Limitations:** The movement slowed in cities because **Khadi** was too expensive for the poor, and there were no alternative Indian institutions (schools/courts) for people to join.
*   **Withdrawal:** Gandhi called off the NCM in **February 1922** after the **Chauri Chaura incident**, where a peaceful protest turned violent and a police station was burned.

### 5. Towards Civil Disobedience
*   **Swaraj Party:** Formed by **C.R. Das and Motilal Nehru** to argue for a return to council politics.
*   **Simon Commission:** A British commission with **no Indian members**. It was greeted with the slogan "**Go Back Simon**" in 1928.
*   **Salt March (1930):** Gandhi chose salt as a symbol of unity because it was used by everyone. He marched **240 miles** from Sabarmati to **Dandi** with 78 volunteers, breaking the salt law on April 6, 1930.

### 6. Civil Disobedience Movement (CDM)
Unlike NCM, the CDM encouraged people to not only refuse cooperation but also to **break colonial laws**.
*   **Participants:**
    *   **Rich Peasants:** Fought against high land revenue.
    *   **Business Class:** Wanted protection against imports; formed the FICCI.
    *   **Women:** Participated in protests and salt manufacturing in large numbers.
*   **Limitations:** 
    *   **Dalits:** Many Dalit leaders, like **B.R. Ambedkar**, demanded separate electorates. This led to the **Poona Pact** (1932), which gave depressed classes reserved seats in provincial councils.
    *   **Communal Divide:** Increased distance between Hindus and Muslims due to the activities of the Hindu Mahasabha and Muslim League.

### 7. The Sense of Collective Belonging
Nationalism was fostered through cultural processes:
*   **Figures and Images:** The image of **Bharat Mata** (first created by Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay and painted by Abanindranath Tagore).
*   **National Song:** *Vande Mataram*.
*   **Folklore:** Revival of Indian folklore by Rabindranath Tagore and Natesa Sastri.
*   **Flags:** The Swadeshi flag (Bengal) and Gandhi’s **Swaraj flag**.
*   **Reinterpretation of History:** Reminding Indians of their glorious past to instill pride.
