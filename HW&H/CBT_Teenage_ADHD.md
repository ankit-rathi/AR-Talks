## For Kid

| Situation                               | Action (What You Should Do)                                                | Consequence                                                           |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Mocking / Making Faces / Provoking      | Pause → Reset → Continue calmly or take a 5-minute cool-down               | Continued behavior = reduced screen/cricket time                      |
| Feeling Angry or Overstimulated         | Use balcony / short walk / quiet reset instead of shouting or running away | Running outside after conflict = no cricket/free time that day        |
| Teasing Little Sister                   | Stop → Hands to yourself → Take reset break if needed                      | Repeated teasing = loss of next reward                                |
| Study Time Starts                       | Complete one study block at a time (25–40 min)                             | Not completing study block = reduced cricket/screen time              |
| Distracted During Study                 | Pause → Drink water / short reset → Restart smaller task                   | Continued distraction = study block ends early                        |
| Going Outside / Public Places           | Use “Silent Body Mode” (normal face, calm hands, no muttering)             | Repeated public behavior = shorter outing / reduced freedom next time |
| Feeling Like Abusing or Making Gestures | Pause → Deep breath → Stay quiet or write it down                          | Continued behavior = reduced privileges                               |
| During Arguments                        | Speak calmly or take a reset break                                         | Escalation = discussion stops until calm                              |
| Completing Tasks Calmly                 | Finish work first, then enjoy cricket/free time                            | Rewards earned through consistency                                    |
| Regaining Control After Mistake         | Say sorry, reset, and continue normally                                    | Faster recovery = trust and more freedom                              |


## For Parents

| Situation                               | What NOT to Do                                           | CBT Script (What to Say)                                                                                    | Parent Action                                     | Consequence (Calm & Predictable)                                 | Goal Being Trained          |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **Mocking / Making Faces / Provoking**  | Don’t shout, insult, argue, or react emotionally         | “You’re overstimulated right now.” → “Pause.” → “You can reset and continue, or take a 5-minute cool-down.” | Stay calm, low voice, minimal words               | Continued mocking = reduced screen/cricket time                  | Pause before impulse        |
| **Escalation During Mocking**           | Don’t continue debate or lecture                         | “We’ll talk when your body is calm.”                                                                        | Disengage briefly                                 | Conversation paused until calm                                   | Emotional regulation        |
| **Running Outside After Conflict**      | Don’t chase emotionally or negotiate while he is outside | “You can be upset. You cannot leave like this.”                                                             | Ensure safety quietly, avoid drama                | Leaving during conflict = no cricket/free time that day          | Autonomy within boundaries  |
| **Preventive Script for Running Away**  | Don’t wait for conflict to create rules                  | “If you feel angry, you can use balcony, downstairs walk, or grandparents with permission.”                 | Discuss during calm time                          | Clear pre-defined rule                                           | Safe emotional outlet       |
| **Sibling Teasing**                     | Don’t compare siblings or shame him                      | “Stop. Hands to yourself.” → “You can sit calmly or take a reset break.”                                    | Interrupt immediately but calmly                  | Repeated teasing = loss of next reward                           | Respecting boundaries       |
| **Post-Teasing Reflection**             | Don’t give long lectures                                 | “What happened?” → “What can you do instead next time?”                                                     | Keep discussion under 1 minute                    | None during reflection                                           | Self-awareness              |
| **Study Refusal**                       | Don’t threaten future failure or compare with others     | “Only 20 minutes.” → “Just Q1–5.” → “You don’t have to like it. You just have to start.”                    | Reduce task size, start small                     | Delay/refusal = reduced cricket/screen time                      | Task initiation             |
| **Distracted / Frenzy During Study**    | Don’t repeatedly say “Sit properly!”                     | “Your brain is distracted. Reset.”                                                                          | Short movement/water break, restart smaller block | Block ends early if behavior continues                           | Focus recovery              |
| **Public Gestures / Mutters / Staring** | Don’t scold publicly or shame                            | “Mode.” or “Reset.”                                                                                         | Use short cue only                                | Repeated behavior = shorter outing / reduced freedom next outing | Public self-regulation      |
| **Before Going Outside**                | Don’t assume he remembers expectations                   | “What mode are we using outside?” → “Silent Body Mode.”                                                     | Make him repeat coping strategy                   | None                                                             | Behavioral priming          |
| **General Escalation**                  | Don’t emotionally overpower him                          | “Pause.” → “We’ll solve this after calm.”                                                                   | Slow tone, fewer words                            | Natural consequence later                                        | Nervous system regulation   |
| **When He Regulates Even Briefly**      | Don’t ignore positive behavior                           | “Good recovery.” / “You controlled yourself there.”                                                         | Praise immediately and specifically               | Positive reinforcement                                           | Identity building           |
| **Daily Study Blocks**                  | Don’t force long hours suddenly                          | “One block at a time.”                                                                                      | Use timer (25–40 min) + visible task              | Reward only after completion                                     | Consistency                 |
| **Universal Rule**                      | Don’t over-explain during emotional state                | “You are responsible for your behavior, even when your feelings are big.”                                   | Calm repetition                                   | Consistent outcome                                               | Accountability + regulation |
